Dr. Emmanuel Yih-Herng Chang is a board-certified ophthalmologist specializing in vitreoretinal diseases and surgery. He currently serves as a Clinical Assistant Professor in the Department of Ophthalmology at Baylor College of Medicine in Houston, Texas. ([bcm.edu](https://www.bcm.edu/people-search/emmanuel-chang-19178?utm_source=openai))

**Overview**

Dr. Chang has an extensive background in both adult and pediatric vitreoretinal diseases, with particular expertise in conditions such as macular degeneration, macular holes, retinal detachments, diabetic retinopathy, vein occlusions, and various pediatric retinal disorders including retinopathy of prematurity, familial exudative vitreoretinopathy, persistent fetal vasculature, Coats’ disease, and ocular trauma. His research focuses on integrating biomedical engineering and nanotechnology to develop novel therapeutics for vitreoretinal diseases. ([retinatexas.com](https://www.retinatexas.com/retina-specialist/emmanuel-y-chang-md-phd-facs/?utm_source=openai))
