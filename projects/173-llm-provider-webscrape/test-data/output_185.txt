Dr. Darin T. Okuda is a distinguished neurologist specializing in multiple sclerosis and other demyelinating disorders of the central nervous system. He currently serves as a Professor in the Department of Neurology at UT Southwestern Medical Center, where he holds multiple leadership roles, including Director of the Neuroinnovation Program and the Multiple Sclerosis and Neuroimmunology Imaging Program. ([profiles.utsouthwestern.edu](https://profiles.utsouthwestern.edu/profile/146752/darin-okuda.html?utm_source=openai))

**Overview**

Dr. Okuda is a clinician-scientist renowned for his contributions to the understanding and management of multiple sclerosis (MS). He is internationally recognized for defining and investigating the radiologically isolated syndrome (RIS), a condition characterized by MRI findings suggestive of MS in individuals without clinical symptoms. His research focuses on developing innovative diagnostic and clinical surveillance metrics, as well as transforming medical decision-making through technology. ([profiles.utsouthwestern.edu](https://profiles.utsouthwestern.edu/profile/146752/darin-okuda.html?utm_source=openai))
